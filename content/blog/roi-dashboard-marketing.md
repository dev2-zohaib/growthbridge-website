# Building a Marketing ROI Dashboard That Decision-Makers Actually Use

Marketing dashboards often fail because they focus on activity instead of outcomes. Leadership teams do not need more charts. They need clarity on what is driving pipeline, what is wasting budget, and where to invest next.

## Start with business questions

A useful dashboard should answer a small set of repeatable questions:
- Which channels are generating qualified demand?
- Where are conversion rates dropping?
- What is the cost to acquire pipeline or customers?
- How fast are experiments improving performance?

## Align reporting to decisions

When dashboards are tied to weekly and monthly decisions, adoption improves. Pair top-line KPIs with the drill-down metrics needed by channel owners.

## Keep iteration simple

Do not wait for perfect attribution. Build a reliable baseline, identify blind spots, and improve reporting over time.
